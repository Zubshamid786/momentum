# Momentum — User Guide

Momentum is your personal productivity companion. It keeps your projects, tasks, and time in one place — and because everything lives in the cloud, your phone, your Mac, and any web browser always show the same, up-to-date information.

**Your app:** https://zubshamid786.github.io/momentum/

---

## Opening Momentum

- **On your phone:** tap the Momentum icon on your home screen (the purple lightning bolt).
- **On your Mac:** open the Momentum app from Applications.
- **Anywhere else:** open the link above in any browser. The first time on a new device it asks for your database address and key — enter them once and it remembers.

Everything stays in sync automatically. Add a task on your phone, and it's on your Mac seconds later.

## The screens, in plain words

### 🏠 Dashboard — your morning starting point
The first thing you see. The **Today at a Glance** card shows how your day is going: a progress ring ("3 of 8 done"), your focus time against your daily goal, your tasks lined up **in time order**, and what you've already completed. Below it: quick stats, charts, and the pulse of every project.

**MITs** (Most Important Tasks): each morning, tap *Set MITs* and pick the 3 things that matter most today. At night, tap *End-of-Day Review* to reflect.

### 📁 Projects — where work lives
Each project holds its tasks on a board with columns: **To Do → In Progress → Blocked → Done**. Drag cards between columns as work progresses.

Tap any task to open its details: due date and time, priority, estimate, subtasks (little checkboxes inside a task), comments, and the time tracker. Two bonus views at the top: **Diagram** (a visual map of the project) and **Mountain** (your progress drawn as a climb to a summit — reach 100% and plant the flag 🚩).

### ⏱ Tracking your time
On any task, press **Start Timer** when you begin working and **Stop** when you're done — Momentum records exactly how long you worked. The running timer always shows at the top of the screen.

Prefer working in focused sprints? Press **Pomodoro** instead: 25 minutes of focus, then a short break, automatically. The full-screen focus view keeps you distraction-free — or tap the minimize button to shrink it into a small corner widget while you move around the app.

Forgot to start the timer? Open the task → **Add manual entry** → type how long you worked (like 1h 30m). Done.

### 📅 Calendar — plan vs reality
See your tasks by month, week, or day. The **day view** is special: it has two lanes side by side — the left lane shows time you *actually* logged, the right lane shows what you *planned* (tasks with a set time appear there automatically). One glance tells you how your plan compares to reality.

### 📓 Journal — a page per day
A clean writing space with one entry per day. It saves as you type. Use it for notes, reflections, or anything on your mind.

### 📊 Reports — where your time went
Charts and totals for today, this week, or this month — by day and by project. The **Weekly Review** (bottom of the sidebar) gives you a summary of your week and lets you write a commitment for the next one.

### 🎯 Goals — the big picture
Set your WIGs (Wildly Important Goals), link them to projects, and watch the scoreboard track your progress week by week.

### 📥 Inbox — the catch-all
Something popped into your head? Press **⌘N** (Mac) or use the Inbox to capture it instantly. Sort it into the right project later.

## Everyday flow (a suggestion)

1. **Morning:** open the Dashboard → Set your 3 MITs → glance at Today at a Glance.
2. **During the day:** start the timer when you work; drag tasks across the board as they progress.
3. **Planning:** give tasks a due date *and time* — they'll appear on your calendar automatically.
4. **Evening:** End-of-Day Review → maybe a journal entry.
5. **Sunday:** Weekly Review → write next week's commitment.

## If something goes wrong

**"It's not loading / spinning forever."**
You're probably offline — Momentum needs internet to reach your cloud data. Reconnect and reload.

**"My phone and Mac show different things."**
Just refresh (pull down on the phone, ⌘R on the Mac). Both read the same database; one of them is simply showing an older copy of the screen.

**"The Mac app asks me to connect."**
Open **Settings → Cloud Sync** and re-enter your database address and key (the same two values you used on your phone).

**"I want to work offline on the Mac."**
Settings → Cloud Sync → **Disconnect** switches the Mac to its own local (offline) copy. Note: changes made offline stay on the Mac only — reconnect to go back to the shared cloud data.

**"I lost my database key."**
Go to turso.tech, sign in, and generate a new token for your database. Enter it on each device once.

## Good to know

- **Your data is yours.** It lives in your own private cloud database — no company account, no subscription, no cost.
- **The key is the lock.** Anyone with your database key can see your data. Don't share it or post it anywhere.
- **The Mac's old data is a backup.** From the day you switched to cloud, the Mac keeps its old local copy frozen — a safety net that never changes.
