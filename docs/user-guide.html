Momentum

User Guide — everything you need, in plain words

Open the app →

Momentum is your personal productivity companion. It keeps your projects, tasks, and time in one place — and because everything lives in the cloud, your phone, your Mac, and any browser always show the same, up-to-date information.

📲Opening Momentum

📱On your phoneTap the purple lightning-bolt icon on your home screen
💻On your MacOpen Momentum from Applications
🌐Anywhere elseOpen the app link in any browser — enter your database key once
✓ Always in syncAdd a task on your phone, and it's on your Mac seconds later. Automatically.

🏠Dashboard — your morning starting point

The first thing you see, built to answer “how is my day going?” in one glance.

The Today at a Glance card shows a progress ring (“3 of 8 done”), your focus time against your daily goal, your tasks lined up in time order, and what you've already completed.

⭐ MITs — Most Important Tasks

Each morning, tap Set MITs and pick the 3 things that matter most today. At night, tap End-of-Day Review to reflect on how it went.

📁Projects — where work lives

Each project holds its tasks on a board with four columns:

To Do→ In Progress→ Blocked→ Done

Drag cards between columns as work progresses. Tap any task to open its details: due date & time, priority, estimate, subtasks (little checkboxes inside a task), comments, and the time tracker.

Two bonus views

🗺 Diagram — a visual map of the whole project.  🏔 Mountain — your progress drawn as a climb to a summit. Reach 100% and plant the flag 🚩.

⏱Tracking your time

On any task, press Start Timer when you begin and Stop when you're done — Momentum records exactly how long you worked. The running timer always shows at the top of the screen.

🍅 Pomodoro

Prefer focused sprints? Press Pomodoro: 25 minutes of focus, then a short break, automatically. The full-screen focus view keeps you distraction-free — or tap minimize to shrink it into a small corner widget while you move around the app.

Forgot the timer?

Open the task → Add manual entry → type how long you worked (like 1h 30m). Done.

📅Calendar — plan vs reality

See your tasks by month, week, or day. The day view is special — two lanes side by side:

🟩 Left lane: time you actually logged  ·  🟦 Right lane: what you planned (tasks with a set time appear automatically)

One glance tells you how your plan compares to reality.

📓Journal — a page per day

A clean writing space, one entry per day, that saves as you type. Use it for notes, reflections, or anything on your mind.

📊Reports — where your time went

Charts and totals for today, this week, or this month — by day and by project. The Weekly Review (bottom of the sidebar) summarizes your week and lets you write a commitment for the next one.

🎯Goals & 📥 Inbox

Goals: set your WIGs (Wildly Important Goals), link them to projects, and watch the scoreboard track your weekly progress.

Inbox: something popped into your head? Press ⌘N on the Mac to capture it instantly. Sort it into the right project later.

🔄A suggested daily flow

Morning — open the Dashboard → set your 3 MITs → glance at Today at a Glance.
During the day — start the timer when you work; drag tasks across the board as they progress.
Planning — give tasks a due date and time — they appear on your calendar automatically.
Evening — End-of-Day Review → maybe a journal entry.
Sunday — Weekly Review → write next week's commitment.

🛟If something goes wrong

“It's not loading / spinning forever”You're probably offline — Momentum needs internet to reach your cloud data. Reconnect and reload.
“My phone and Mac show different things”Just refresh (pull down on the phone, ⌘R on the Mac). Both read the same database; one is showing an older copy of the screen.
“The Mac app asks me to connect”Open Settings → Cloud Sync and re-enter your database address and key — the same two values you used on your phone.
“I want to work offline on the Mac”Settings → Cloud Sync → Disconnect switches to a local offline copy. Changes made offline stay on the Mac only — reconnect to return to shared cloud data.
“I lost my database key”Go to turso.tech, sign in, generate a new token for your database, and enter it on each device once.

💡Good to know

Your data is yoursIt lives in your own private cloud database — no company account, no subscription, no cost.
The key is the lockAnyone with your database key can see your data. Don't share it or post it anywhere.
The Mac's old data is a backupFrom the day you switched to cloud, the Mac keeps its old local copy frozen — a safety net that never changes.
